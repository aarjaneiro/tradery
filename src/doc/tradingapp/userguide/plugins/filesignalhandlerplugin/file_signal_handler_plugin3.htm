The new tab has the same name as the plug-in configuration in this case: "Test file signal handler" and it shows various session statistics regarding trading signals and simulated trades.

The signals information is displayed in real time, as trading signals are generated. The session statistics are shown when the session has completed.

Statistics

The Session Statistics table contains 4 groups of stats: all positions (long+short), only long, only short and buy & hold. For each of these groups, various stats are shown: trade statistics which refer to various position counts and percentages, performance statistics, slippage statistics and commission statistics.

Performance statistics for, or which include, open positions, will calculate gain based on the most recent price information. This is in fact unrealized gain, calculated by subtracting the entry price from the most recent price which is the closing price for the last available bar or tick for longs, or vice-versa, for shorts.

Total gain will include realized gain (for closed positions) as well as unrealized gain (for open positions).

Buy & Hold stats are useful in comparing the performance of a trading system with the traditional buy & hold strategy. Buy & hold stats are generated by creating fictitious trades for all symbols, where the entry price is the first available price (opening price on the first bar or tick) and the exit price is the last available price (closing price on the last bar or tick). The same amount, equal to the initial capital divided by the number of symbols, is invested in each of the buy & hold positions. The number of initial shares is then equal to that amount divided by the position opening price. If the initial capital is not high enough, some of the buy & hold positions may not be created, as the calculated share amount turn out to be 0.

Once the session statistics are available, they can be saved to a CSV file by clicking on the button "Save".

Note: future releases will offer many more options in terms of generated stats, including equity curve in numeric and graphical form, as well as predefined position sizing strategies.
